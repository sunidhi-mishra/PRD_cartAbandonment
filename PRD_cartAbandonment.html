01

Problem Statement

65% of users abandon their carts before purchasing, with a significant drop-off specifically at the payment step. Internal data and funnel analytics point to four competing hypotheses, each backed by a different team:

💳
Hypothesis 1
Unreliable payment flows (frequent UPI/card failures)
💵
Hypothesis 2
Lack of visible Cash on Delivery (COD) options early in the checkout
💸
Hypothesis 3
Unexpected additional fees (shipping or taxes) surfacing only at the final step
⏱️
Hypothesis 4
Slow page loads on the payment screen
Business Impact

Customers experience surprise or frustration, eroding trust and leading to lost business. The opportunity is material: recovering even a portion of this lost GMV directly lifts top-line revenue.

🗺️
02

High-Level Approach

Pursue a phased solution.

Phase 1 · 4 Weeks
Diagnostic + Quick Wins

A focused, 4-week diagnostic and quick-win sprint: instrument the funnel granularly, immediately fix low-hanging issues (fee transparency, COD visibility), and optimize obvious technical bottlenecks (load time)—all based on minimal engineering lift.

Phase 2 · Follows Phase 1
Full Checkout Redesign

A full checkout redesign, but only after collecting hard evidence on exact failure points from Phase 1. This minimizes risk of wasted resources and directly targets the hypothesized causes.

Narrative & Personas

P
Priya, 28 — Frequent Shopper, Low Tolerance for Price Surprises, High Intent Buyer
Marketing Executive · Bengaluru · Shops frequently across categories
Common use case
Discovery
Priya discovers a phone case priced at ₹349 — it fits her budget. She proceeds to checkout with clear purchase intent.
Payment step — surprise
An additional ₹99 shipping fee and ₹42 tax are revealed for the first time — increasing the total by ~40%.
Abandonment
Priya had already mentally committed to the original price. The late-stage increase creates a perception of hidden costs. She abandons and switches to a competitor with upfront pricing.
Behavioral insight Users anchor to the first price they see. Any late deviation feels like deception, not adjustment.
Root cause This is not a pricing issue — it is a trust breakdown.
R
Rahul, 34 — Low-Trust, Occasional Buyer Making First High-Value Purchase
Schoolteacher · Gorakhpur · Rare shopper · First high-value online purchase
Edge case
Context
Rahul is purchasing a water purifier (>₹5,000) — his first high-value online purchase. He is highly cautious about payment risk and actively looking for COD availability.
Interface failure
COD is not clearly visible until the final payment screen — and even then, it is buried below other options. Unable to confirm availability, Rahul cannot get the reassurance he needs.
Abandonment
Rahul assumes COD is not supported and abandons the purchase — not because he couldn't pay, but because the interface failed to reassure him at the right moment.
Behavioral insight For trust-sensitive users, absence of reassurance is interpreted as absence of the feature itself.
Root cause This is not a payment failure — it is a failure of reassurance at the right moment.

Synthesis: what's actually broken

Checkout is not just a transaction flow — it is a trust negotiation system.

Key Flows

Fee transparency flow
1
Screen: Product Detail Page (PDP)
User seesProduct price + inline shipping estimate ("Delivery: ₹49 to 560001") and estimated tax ("+₹28 GST") below the price. If no pincode is detected, a prompt appears: "Enter pincode to see delivery charges."
User doesEnters pincode if missing, then clicks "Add to Cart."
ResultFee estimate is stored in session. Product added to cart.
↓
2
Screen: Cart Page
User seesItemized breakdown — product subtotal, per-item shipping, tax, and order total. A "How is this calculated?" tooltip is available.
User doesReviews total, proceeds to checkout.
ResultNo new fee information introduced at payment — total matches exactly.
↓
3
Screen: Checkout / Payment
User seesIdentical total as cart page. No new line items.
User doesSelects payment method and completes purchase.

Edge cases — fee transparency flow

User adds or removes items after fee estimate is shown: Fee recalculates in real-time on the cart page. A subtle "Delivery charges updated" toast appears.
User changes delivery pincode after estimate is shown: Full fee re-fetch triggers. If new pincode has higher shipping, a banner reads "Delivery charges updated for new address." If lower or free, a positive confirmation appears.
Pincode API is down: Inline estimate is replaced with "Shipping cost will be confirmed at checkout" in muted text. No hard error shown. Checkout is not blocked.
Item in cart is not eligible for delivery to pincode: A clear inline warning on the cart page reads "This item cannot be delivered to [pincode]. Remove it or change your delivery address to proceed."
COD discovery flow
1
Screen: Cart Page
User seesA green badge below the delivery address field: "✓ Cash on Delivery available for [pincode]." If COD is not available, the badge is absent — no negative messaging shown.
User doesConfirms delivery address and proceeds to checkout.
ResultUser arrives at payment already knowing COD is an option.
↓
2
Screen: Payment Method Selection
User seesCOD listed as the first payment option (above UPI and card) for COD-eligible users, with a note: "Pay ₹[total] in cash at delivery. No advance needed."
User doesSelects COD and confirms order.
ResultOrder placed. Confirmation screen shown with delivery timeline and COD reminder.

Edge cases — COD discovery flow

User is not COD-eligible for their pincode: COD badge is simply not shown on the cart. COD does not appear as a payment option. No explicit "COD not available" message — avoids friction and confusion.
User changes pincode to a COD-ineligible area after seeing the badge: Badge disappears with a subtle update. If user had selected COD on the payment screen and then changes address, COD option is removed and user is prompted to select an alternate method with a contextual message: "COD is not available for your new address. Please choose another payment method."
Pincode API timeout during COD check: COD badge is not shown (fail-safe — never falsely promise COD). A retry is triggered silently in the background. If resolved before the user reaches payment, COD appears there.
Cart contains a mix of COD-eligible and COD-ineligible items: COD is not offered. A tooltip on the payment screen explains: "One or more items in your cart are not eligible for COD."
Payment failure recovery flow
1
Screen: Payment Screen
User seesPayment methods listed: UPI (default, pre-selected), Debit/Credit Card, Net Banking, COD (if eligible). A security badge and "100% secure checkout" label are visible.
User doesEnters UPI ID or scans QR and confirms payment.
ResultPayment request sent to UPI gateway.
↓
2
Screen: Payment Processing
User seesLoading spinner with message "Processing your payment… do not press back."
ResultGateway responds with success or failure within 30 seconds.
↓
3
Screen: Payment Failure
User seesA non-alarming failure message: "Payment didn't go through. This happens sometimes — your money is safe." Immediately below: three clearly labeled CTAs — "Try UPI again," "Pay by Card," and (if COD-eligible) "Pay Cash on Delivery." A fourth option: "Need help? Contact support."
User doesSelects alternate method.
ResultUser is taken directly to the relevant payment form — no return to the full payment selection screen.

Edge cases — payment failure recovery flow

UPI timeout (no response within 30 seconds): System treats it as a failure. User is shown the same recovery screen. Backend simultaneously checks if any debit was made — if yes, auto-initiates refund and informs user: "We noticed a deduction. A refund will be processed in 3–5 business days."
User attempts card payment after UPI failure, and card also fails: After second consecutive failure, the recovery screen changes tone: "Having trouble? Try COD or contact support." Reduces cognitive load — don't ask user to try a third method without guidance.
User closes the app during payment processing: On re-opening, if payment status is unknown, the app shows an order status banner: "We're confirming your payment. Check back in a few minutes." Links to order tracking page. Does not create a duplicate order.
COD is not available and both UPI and card fail: Recovery screen shows only "Try UPI again," "Pay by Card (different card)," and "Contact Support." No dead end — support CTA is always visible.
User's UPI app is not installed: UPI option is grayed out with tooltip "UPI app not detected on this device." Only card and COD (if eligible) are active.

Key Logic

Rules

Click each rule to expand the detail.

Shipping/tax fees must be calculated for user pincode + item weight, shown at PDP/cart — not deferred to checkout.

Applies when: PDP loads with a pincode present (geo/IP or user-entered).
Example: Bengaluru 560001 sees ₹49 shipping; Delhi 110011 sees ₹0.

COD availability must be checked by pincode when user lands on cart, not just at payment.

Applies when: Cart loads full.
Example: If pincode 560001 is COD-eligible, badge is green; if not, badge is red or omitted.

After first UPI/card failure, show alternate options immediately (don't force same-method retry).

Applies when: Payment error received.
Example: UPI failure — immediately show UPI retry + alternate methods + COD if available.

Must achieve first contentful paint <2s on 4G for payment step.

Applies when: Navigating from cart to payment.
Example: On Moto G51 4G, Chrome, with JS bundles lazy loaded.

Edge cases

What happens if pincode cannot be resolved (API downtime)?
Show "Fee estimate unavailable. Will be confirmed at checkout" — with retry link.
What if user changes pincode/cart after estimate?
Dynamically update fee/COD badge accordingly.
Concurrent user action:
Cart update during slow fee fetch must queue/fetch new estimate, never stale data.

Experiment design

TypeA/B test (Phase 2, payment failure recovery flow)
Sample size~500,000 users per arm for statistical significance (est. based on baseline CCR uplift of 15%)
Success criteriaCCR improves by at least 10%, statistically significant at 95% confidence, no drop in guardrails
Gray zone decision rule

If CCR improvement is directionally positive but below the 10% threshold (e.g., 5–9% lift at 95% confidence), the decision is not an automatic rollback. The PM and Data Lead jointly evaluate: (a) whether the improvement is statistically significant at 90% confidence, (b) whether secondary metrics (payment drop-off, support tickets) corroborate the direction, and (c) whether the cost of delay outweighs shipping a smaller-than-expected gain. A documented decision memo is required before proceeding or pausing.

Dependencies & contingencies

Payments API: Rely on Payments Team for real-time failure detection + alternate payment APIs
Contingency: Fallback to simple failure screen with messaging if APIs delayed
Logistics API: Real-time pincode lookup for fee estimates/COD
Contingency: Cache fallback or user-retry flows
Escalation trigger
Logistics must confirm API readiness and provide a sandbox environment by end of Week 1. If this commitment is not met by Day 5 of Week 1, the PM escalates immediately to the Engineering Director and activates the contingency plan: display a static estimated fee range based on category-level averages (not pincode-level), with a disclaimer 'Exact shipping cost confirmed at checkout.' This fallback ships on the same Week 3 timeline regardless of Logistics API status.

Escalation path

Inter-team blockers (e.g., API delays from Logistics or Payments) escalate to the PM within 24 hours, then to the Engineering Director if unresolved in 48 hours.
Scope disputes between teams are arbitrated by the PM. If unresolved in 3 business days, escalate to the CPO.
Guardrail metric breaches (AOV drop, fraud spike) trigger an immediate rollback decision owned jointly by PM and the Data Lead.
